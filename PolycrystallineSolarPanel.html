POLYCRYSTALLINE SOLAR PANEL

Polycrystalline solar panels are made using multiple silicon crystals rather than a single crystal structure. Here's what constitutes a polycrystalline solar panel:

What do they consist of?


  1. Silicon Wafers: These panels are made of multiple silicon wafers.
  2. Silicon Cells: Each silicon wafer is then treated to create photovoltaic cells.
  3. Encapsulation: The cells are typically encapsulated between multiple layers of protective materials
  4. Frame and Junction Box: Polycrystalline solar panels are housed in a durable frame, usually made of aluminum, which provides structural support and facilitates installation.

How do we DISPOSE them?


  1. Segrating the components:Separate and recycle plastic components if possible. Some recycling facilities accept certain types of plastics.
  2. Transport and Preparation: If you're transporting the panels yourself, ensure safe handling to prevent breakage or damage. .
  3. Appliances should be recycled by facilities that safely remove hazardous components before shredding and recycling. Technicians can recover oil and other hazardous materials.
  4. Recycle Through Manufacturers: Recycle metal components by taking them to a recycling facility.Glass can be recycled. Ensure it is separated and taken to a recycling facility.
  5. Retailer Take-Back Programs: Inquire with electronics retailers or manufacturers about take-back programs. Some companies have recycling initiatives in place, and they may provide options for returning old CRT devices.
  6. Do Not Dump: Avoid dumping in regular trash bins or landfills. The hazardous materials in these devices can leach into the environment, posing risks to soil and water.
